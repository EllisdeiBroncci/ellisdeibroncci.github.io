Fresher's Accommodation

This article is about a few lessons that I learnt from living in student accommodation in my first year of university, as well as the experience I had that led to them.

Fresher's Accommodation

My stay in student accommodation was in White Rose View in Leeds, in the 2022/2023 academic year. Like most student’s, this was my first real life away from home, so of course I started off with a real preparation stage. I spent ages going through places like Ikea and home bargains searching for the cheapest essentials I could find, and slowly adding them to a huge collection of things that I would be taking to move away with. In hindsight, this wasn’t the best idea. I was moving into Leeds city centre, and things like bleach and cleaning supplies you can really find everywhere, and at the end of the day it’s just taking up weight in which you could otherwise use in something else.


My second lesson was immediate and harsh, yet unnoticed for a long time. When you first move out you understand that you’re essentially wholly responsible for yourself, but it’s only after you stay away from home for a long time that you truly understand what that means. When you have spare and free time when you are living by yourself, you almost need to check that you’re actually free. There is always laundry to do, and there is always cleaning to do, and if even those two are done, you’re probably best off beginning a batch cook, or doing something else to save you time in the future. Staying away from home is the harshest and biggest teacher of personal time management. You’re best off not having a routine for this stuff, and just doing it at the first instance it needs doing, or the first instance you can do it, as whilst being academic you’re bombarded with study plans and time organization, domestic life doesn’t really work like that, it’s more a service to yourself. It needs doing when it needs doing, and to a great extent, routines and schedules hold you back more than they free you in this domain.
