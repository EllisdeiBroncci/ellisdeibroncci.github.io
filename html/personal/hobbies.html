Cooking plays an important role in my life, especially since it was my job for two years, here I talk about some of the essentials of a kitchen.

On Personal Culinary Practice

Cooking is a part of our lives that is both a blessing and a curse. Especially in the modern world, where there are so many other things to do than surviving, it’s not a surprise that most people find it a chore, which they’d much rather have someone else do for them, or just not bother with it entirely. Personally, I believe that cooking can range from anywhere between extremely complicated and difficult, to satisfyingly simple, with ends of the spectrum fitting in for your mood of the day. Sometimes, we either don’t have time to cook or we simply just don’t feel like it. Other times, it’s imperative that we make that extra effort, and we'll do as much as that is in our power to make the dish perfect. So how can we strike a balance with cooking being between the two? Here’s what I’ve learnt so far.


Ensure that you have the right equipment for the job. Fundamentally, you need a sharp knife to handle all of your chopping, paired with a good, preferably wooden, chopping board. I’ve personally found a lot of knife dulling occurs simply because of the surface you chop on, and wood is best for maintaining your blade. Make sure you have a decently sized wooden chopping board, and a slightly smaller plastic one for small bites and chopping raw meats on, or anything that might leak out onto the chopping board. You’ll also want means of cooking on a stove. Assuming you already have some form of stove, you’ll want a frying pan and a sauce pan. Along with these you’ll want means of stirring, with a maurice for my preference, as well as a medium sized whisk for anything that’s more viscous. You’ll also need basic cutlery, that is a knife, fork, tablespoon, teaspoon along with a large plate, a small plate and a bowl, however these are for serving and don’t really come under this section, but you can use these things as kitchen tools as well.
