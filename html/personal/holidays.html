Switzerland: The Helvetti

Switzerland was my choice of destination for my summer holiday in 2023, and in this article I reflect my time whilst being there, as well as some of the best lifestyle changes I’ve made since discovering the land of the Swiss.

Switzerland: The Helvetti

Setting off on the train from Leeds, I flew out of Gatwick to Geneva airport, arriving in the afternoon, only to be greeted with the most magnificent sun I’ve ever felt on my skin. As I walked to the city centre with my friend, we had our first proper look at Geneva. The water was such an incredible blue that we both knew then and there at some point we had to swim in it, but for now with all of our baggage, we’d have to leave it for another day. From there we crossed over the river and explored the old city, which noticeably had a completely different vibe from the rest of the city. Whilst most of the modern parts of the city were made up of high street stores and newly laid cobbles that facilitated tram transport, the old town sat on a hill that led to essentially what could be described as an immediate cliff edge. This meant that all throughout moving through the town, you were either going uphill, or downhill.


Once our time window was up we took a train through Lausanne to Leysin, which is where our accommodation was. For once in my life I picked up a newspaper and read through all of it. I found it far more satisfying than reading anything online, as the paper was physically there, and the smell of the newspaper is iconic. There’s something about reading something on a physical page which is so much more memorizing and real than it is reading it off a computer screen.

The next day we made our way down into the valley to take a dip in the water, and to our surprise it was extremely cold. This is because the lake is only supplied by the mountain snow melting and making its way down the mountain into the lake. The water heats up on it’s way down, but by the time it makes it to the lake it’s still nearing freezing. Of course this didn’t deter us from diving in from the rocks of Chateau de Chillon, and exposing ourselves to what must’ve been around 10 degree celsius water, which begins to numb your skin after two minutes.
