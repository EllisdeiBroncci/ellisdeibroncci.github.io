Favourites

BASh
Bourne Again Shell, which I was taught during my induction at university, I which I continued to use throughout my course, learning how to make executable files that would compile my code and make the necessary arrangements to support my files.
C
Perhpas my favourite programming languages, not because of how awkard it is to debug at time and write code in, but because of how specific it is, allowing the programmer to fine tune and adjust their programs to the exact specific needs, I believe what many people see as C's drawbacks, only comes from it's advantages.
C++
The younger brother of C, developing the object oriented elements of C, breaking of the yoke of procedural and straight into a different paradigm. Whilst still maintaining many of the advantages of C, it gives a programmer greater complexity to work with to form form advanced solutions for different fields.
Java
Whilst many are starting to question Java's place as a dominant languages, it still forms the backbone of most of the object oriented paradigm, and has helped produce universal programs that work on many systems, and any language that may take it's crown, will have to inheret many of it's customs and way before fully being able to take it's spot.
